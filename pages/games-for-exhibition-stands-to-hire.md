# **How to Entice Guests & Visitors to Your Exhibition Booth?**

Have you attended many events in the past where companies or organisations are offering specific products to specific sectors or industries?

Then you know that these events can be fun but there can also be some boring aspects to them too. There are always those tables or booths that are set up with products, but after a while, you skip past them.

There can be many incentives to entice potential buyers to come to your table, booth, or display, but we suggest interactive trade show booth games that anyone can play.

## **Drawing Attention to Your Exhibition or Booth**

It can hard to sell your products or services at an exhibition, or even entice someone to come to your booth. People may be hesitant in sharing their personal information for a prize draw.

You can certainly hand out cards or brochures, but exhibition games may be what really attracts the attention at your display booth.

Once you have their attention, and perhaps even offered a small “prize” or “gift”, potential buyers will be happier and more interested in what your company offers.

And the more interest, the greater chances you have at potential buyers signing up or purchasing your products and services in the future. Isn’t that much better than saying hi to visitors as they walk on past?

### **Should I Hire Exhibition Stand Games for My Company?**

If you’re having any concerns, you shouldn’t. All types of businesses and companies can benefit from exhibition games hire. You can draw people to your booth by leading them to the fun games you are offering.

The games can be just for fun, or you can offer a prize if they reach a specific goal. You can offer a small participatory prize just for playing.

Once they’ve done their play, you can offer them a credit card or brochure for your services or products. Since you’ve had a moment to bond, they’ll be more likely to stop and find out what it is that you do.

### **Where Should I Place the Exhibition Game?**

If you’re wondering where you should place the game, it should be near to your exhibition booth or display, so visitors understand what company it’s with. Ideally, people should have to walk past your table, so your game should be in a prominent spot where it can be seen, but people will know it’s also connected to your business or company.

When people are done playing your game, they need to walk past your main table before exiting. This will give you an opportunity to hand them information about your products or services.
