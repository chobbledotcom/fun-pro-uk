## Planning Your Corporate Event Entertainment

### Choosing Games for Your Audience

Corporate audiences are wonderfully diverse, and successful [event entertainment](/corporate-entertainment/) needs to work for everyone attending. We consider factors like age range, physical abilities, personality types, and professional relationships when recommending entertainment options.

Some colleagues thrive on competitive challenges, whilst others prefer collaborative or observational activities. The key is providing a range of options that allow everyone to participate at their comfort level whilst still creating opportunities for interaction and relationship building.

### Balancing Competitive and Inclusive Activities

Competition can be a powerful motivator, but it needs to be handled thoughtfully in corporate settings. We design activities that channel competitive instincts positively, creating shared experiences rather than awkward divisions. This might mean team-based challenges rather than individual competitions, or activities where different skills contribute to success.

The formality level of your event affects entertainment choices significantly. London venues present unique challenges that need careful consideration during the planning process. Many prestigious venues have limited access times, noise restrictions, or space constraints that affect entertainment choices.
