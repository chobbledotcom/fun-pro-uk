  ## Step-by-Step Planning Guide

  ### 1. Set Your Date and Budget

  Give yourself at least 6–8 weeks of lead time. Summer weekends are the most popular choice, but autumn dates work well too — especially if you're using an indoor venue. Set a realistic budget early: a typical company fun day for 100–200 people, with 4–6 games, catering and basic décor, usually falls between £1,500 and £4,000 depending on what you include.

  ### 2. Choose Your Venue

  If you've got outdoor space at your workplace, that's often the simplest option — no venue hire costs and everyone knows how to get there. Otherwise, look for parks, sports clubs or community halls that can accommodate your numbers.

  Key considerations:
  *   **Access for delivery vehicles** — we'll need to get equipment in and out
  *   **Power supply** — most games need a standard 13A socket
  *   **Shelter or indoor fallback** — even in summer, you need a Plan B
  *   **Parking and public transport** — make it easy for families to arrive

  ### 3. Plan for the Weather

  British weather is unpredictable. If you're outdoors, have a wet weather contingency. Many of our games work both indoors and outdoors, so a village hall or sports centre on standby can save the day. We can advise which games are suitable for outdoor use and which are indoor-only.

  ### 4. Choose Activities for All Ages

  A family fun day means exactly that — from toddlers to grandparents. Mix your activities to cover different age groups and energy levels:

  **For younger children:** [Giant Connect 4](/products/giant-connect-4-game-hire), [Giant Jenga](/products/giant-jenga-hire) and [Crazy Golf](/products/crazy-golf) are easy to understand and satisfying to play at any age.

  **For older children and teenagers:** [Dance Machines](/products/dance-machine), [Racing Simulators](/products/racing-car-simulators) and [Retro Arcade Games](/products/retro-arcade-games) draw this age group like magnets. Competitive games like [Batak Lite](/products/batak-lite) are popular with teens who want to beat each other's scores.

  **For adults:** [Table Football](/products/football-tables), [Air Hockey](/products/air-hockey-table-hire) and [Casino Tables](/products/casino-table-hire) give grown-ups their own entertainment. [Photo Booths](/categories/photo-booths-and-magic-mirrors) appeal to every age group and create lasting memories.

  **For everyone:** [Prize Wheels](/products/prize-wheel) and [Prize Cranes](/products/prize-crane-arcade-grabber) are universal crowd-pleasers. Add small prizes for children and vouchers or gifts for adults.

  ### 5. Think About Food and Drink

  Food is a big part of any fun day. We can supply [fun food options](/categories/fun-foods) alongside your games — candy floss carts, popcorn machines, and pick 'n' mix stands all add to the atmosphere and save you sourcing separate catering for snacks.

  For main meals, consider a BBQ, hog roast or street food van. Build a lunch break into your schedule rather than expecting people to eat and play at the same time.

  ### 6. Create a Simple Schedule

  Don't over-plan, but do give the day some structure:

  *   **Arrival and free play** (30–45 minutes) — let people settle in and explore
  *   **Any organised activities** — if you're doing team competitions or speeches, schedule them mid-morning or early afternoon
  *   **Lunch break** — a proper sit-down break keeps energy up
  *   **Free play continues** — let people enjoy the games at their own pace
  *   **Wind down** — prize-giving or final activities before the end

  ### 7. Promote the Day

  Get your team excited in advance. Share a preview of the games on your internal channels, create a simple poster or email with the date, time and what to expect. If children are invited, let parents know what age-appropriate activities will be available so they can plan accordingly.

# Organising a Family Fun Day

A well-planned family fun day brings your team together in a way that no office event can match. Children running between games, colleagues discovering who's secretly competitive at Giant Jenga, partners and families finally putting faces to names — it's the kind of day people talk about for months afterwards.

But getting it right takes some thought. We've helped companies across the UK plan family fun days since 2009, and this guide covers what we've learned about making them work — from choosing the right mix of activities to handling the inevitable British weather.
