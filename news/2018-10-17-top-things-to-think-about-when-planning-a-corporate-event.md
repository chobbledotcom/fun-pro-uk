When it comes to planning a [corporate event](/categories/corporate-entertainment) there is lots to think about from the venue to the theme, sending out invitations to staffing. But don't worry as we have put together a list of the important things to remember when planning an event.

**What is the purpose of the event?**

It is easy to get carried away when planning an event from thinking about things such as the aesthetics of the event, but ensuring that you stick to the purpose of the event is critical for its success. A simple way of doing this is to set out aims/objectives for the event before you start, whether it is a product launch that you wish to share with your customers or an awards ceremony to reward your employees. Without having structured aims your event is likely to be costly without huge effectiveness.

**How to ensure your guests hear about your event?**

Different groups of people will respond better to different forms of media when getting the information out about your event. Many more people are using social media and emails to receive invitations to events than ever before. But this might not be the correct communication method for all events and audiences. So it is important for any event organiser to analyse the target audience for their event to ensure the time and money put into marketing your event to attendees is effective.

**How do you keep your attendees engaged?**

Depending on the type of event you are organising and the objectives you've set, you will change the entertainment you provide.

For a conference you may want to consider a high-profile speaker in the topic of the conference.

If you venue has special features such as being a sports ground, why not offer the chance of a tour.

Alternatively, get your audience engaging online through a discussion on Facebook or show your audience's tweets on a big screen.

**Have you considered all the areas of logistics?**

Although these may seem like small problems to solve, if you don't tackle them correctly they could become a much larger issue during or after your event. For instance, the flow of traffic and parking contributes to the safety of all your attendees. Alongside having enough and well-trained staff who are prepared for any situation. Other areas of logistics that organisers should consider (but not limited to): signage, food, sponsorship, facilities, set up/build plan and audio-visual elements.

**Do you need a de**brief**?**

Debriefs are so important to ensure that you managed to hit the aims you set before the event. It is a great chance to analyse what worked well within your event, anywhere from dé'cor or location of the venue. But it is also critical in giving all those involved with organising the event the chance to discuss what they felt didn't go to plan such as traffic flow to the event schedule.
