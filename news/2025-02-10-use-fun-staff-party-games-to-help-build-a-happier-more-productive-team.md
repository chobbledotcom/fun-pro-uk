# 10 February 25 - Use Fun Staff Party Games to Help Build a Happier, More Productive Team

Staff parties are a great way to encourage [team bonding](/news/the-best-ideas-for-team-bonding-activities-in-the-workplace/#content) and make everyone feel included, and build stronger relationships at work. The type of parties you throw at your company say a lot about the culture you’ve created or are trying to create, so it’s important to think about this when planning the activities you’d like to include.

Whether it’s fun staff party games that allow everyone to show their competitive side, or something a bit more formal where your team can mingle and get to know each other outside of work, the key to a good staff party lies in the planning.

In this post, we’re going to share our top tips for throwing an unforgettable staff party to show your team that you appreciate them and what they do, and in turn, build a happier, more fulfilled and productive team.

## The Benefits of Staff Party Games:

Staff party games are a lot of fun and offer the perfect way to bring your team together outside of work and show their fun side. Here are some of the things that make staff party games so popular among employees:

### They encourage team bonding

Just because people work together, doesn’t necessarily mean they have gotten to know each other other than to say hello when passing by in the corridor or in the occasional team meeting.

[This 2022 study](https://hbr.org/2020/07/true-friends-at-work) by the Harvard Business Review found that genuine work-based friendships not only benefit employees, but can have a positive impact on the business itself due to increased productivity and morale.

You may even have experienced this yourself; having people at work that you can chat to each day, go for lunch, and have a laugh makes going to work more enjoyable and less tedious, so it’s the same for your staff.

When at work each day, people are mostly kept within their own teams, making it difficult to form friendships outside their team or department. Staff parties, and especially those that include interactive activities like staff party games are great ways to get people mixing.

[Another study found](https://www.stylist.co.uk/life/careers/work-best-friend-research/917923) that more than half of UK workers directly credit their current or previous jobs with meeting some of their most important friends. Of course, we can’t say that staff parties are the main reason they met and formed a friendship, but it’s certainly clear that certain aspects of company culture make it easier for employees to connect and bond with each other.

### They’re inclusive

With so many options to choose from and with something for everyone, staff party games are designed to be inclusive and take into consideration things like physical ability, whether someone is extroverted or introverted, and many can even help with managing things like language barriers which is especially important when you’re leading a multicultural team.

### They boost staff morale

It’s just a fact of life that we all have to work, but with over [one third of our lives](https://www.gettysburg.edu/news/stories?id=79db7b34-630c-4f49-ad32-4ab9ea48e72b) spent working, the profession we choose should be something that is enjoyable, or at the very least, not something that we hate.

Many companies will throw the traditional annual Christmas party, but from the perspective of the staff, it can often feel like nothing more than a tick box exercise with very little thought or effort put in.

When you truly care about building a strong and positive company culture and take the time to think about fun staff party ideas your team will genuinely enjoy and want to participate in, then your employees will feel more appreciated at work, staff retention rates will be higher, and morale will be better overall.

### They improve communication

One of the most challenging aspects of managing a team is navigating the different personalities and communication styles in a way that everyone feels supported and heard.

Group activities like staff party games require teamwork and communication which will then filter through into helping them communicate more effectively in the workplace.

## Tips For Organising Your Next Staff Party:

Planning a staff party that people enjoy takes a good level of planning and organising to make sure that it goes smoothly and that your employees are talking about it for all the right reasons.

Here are some of our top staff party planning tips:

### Set a budget

The first step is to think about how much you can afford for things like the venue, games, food, and other essentials.

It’s also a good idea to shop around so that you can balance cost-effectiveness with quality to get the best value for your money.

### Choose a suitable venue

When looking for a location for your staff party, you should look for one that can comfortably accommodate the amount of people in your team, as well as space for any planned activities like party games, stalls, or photo booths. You should also consider the time of year and if you plan to have any outdoor activities.

### Decide on themes and entertainment

Staff parties with a specific theme like a [retro arcade](/products/retro-arcade-games/#content) night, carnival or a decade-specific theme is a great way to get people involved in the activities and show their personalities outside of work.

Once you’ve chosen your theme, you can pick things to bring it to life like music, lighting, activities, and decor.

### Consider different needs and preferences

When planning something like a staff party or event, it’s important to think about the fact that your team consists of people with a variety of needs and preferences.

For example, if you have staff members who are neurodivergent, or have a physical difficulty, then choosing activities that could be overwhelming for them, or a venue that doesn’t have wheelchair access is probably going to exclude some of your team from attending.

You should also consider that some people are more introverted or extroverted than others, and while you can’t accommodate 100% of everyone’s needs and preferences, aiming to make your staff party as inclusive as possible will make it easier for people to attend and also make them feel that the company really cares about them and appreciates the work they do, in turn leading to increased morale and higher productivity
