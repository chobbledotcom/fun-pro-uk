Yet when planned thoughtfully, team building days can genuinely strengthen working relationships, boost morale, and give colleagues a chance to connect outside the usual pressures of deadlines and meetings.

The difference between a memorable team building event and one that falls flat often comes down to the activities themselves. Games that create natural competition, accommodate different personalities, and feel genuinely enjoyable tend to bring people together far more effectively than activities that feel contrived or exclude certain team members.

## **What Makes Team Building Events Successful?**

![](/images/optima-health-fun-pro-uk.png)

The most effective team building days share several characteristics that set them apart from forgettable corporate exercises and forced fun..

### **Voluntary participation**

This is perhaps the most overlooked factor. When activities feel optional rather than mandatory, engagement tends to be higher. Games with visible scoreboards and competitive elements naturally draw people in without the awkwardness of forced participation. Someone might hover on the sidelines initially, then find themselves drawn into a [reaction game](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/category/batak) challenge when they see a colleague's score to beat.

### **Inclusivity**

This matters enormously. A [team building event](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/pages/team-building-ideas) where half the group sits out because they can't participate achieves the opposite of its intended purpose. Activities that work for different ages, abilities, and comfort levels ensure everyone can join in, whether that's a seated [arcade game](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/category/arcade-games) or a giant strategy game that requires thinking rather than physical prowess.

### **Measurable engagement**

Using things like leaderboards, scores, and friendly competition creates talking points that extend well beyond the event itself. When someone sets the office record on a reaction challenge, that becomes part of the team's shared history in a way that vague bonding exercises simply cannot match.

## **Planning Your Team Building Day**

Before selecting specific activities, it's worth considering the practical elements that shape what's possible.

### **Timing and format**

These vary considerably depending on your objectives. A one-hour energiser between [conference](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/pages/conference-idea) sessions serves a different purpose than a full-day away day. For shorter slots, high-impact games that require minimal explanation work well. Longer events benefit from variety, with multiple activities allowing people to try different things throughout the day.

### **Venue considerations**

The venue will always influence which activities are feasible. Office-based events suit compact games that don't require extensive space, whilst hotel conference rooms or dedicated venues can accommodate larger setups. If you're working with limited floor space, games like [cash grabbers](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/category/exhibition-games/3/cash-grabber-machine-hire) or [racing car simulators](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/category/arcade-games/5/racing-car-simulators) deliver high engagement without needing a sports hall.

### **Group size**

The size of your event and the number of attendees affect both logistics and activity selection. Smaller teams of ten to fifteen people might rotate through stations as a single group, whilst larger gatherings work better with simultaneous activities running across the venue. Tournament formats help larger groups stay engaged, with qualifying rounds building towards finals that everyone can watch.

### **Budget planning**

This requires balancing ambition with practicality. [Corporate event hire](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/pages/office-and-corporate-entertainment) typically provides better value than it might initially appear, particularly when you factor in the planning time saved and the quality of equipment provided. A single well-chosen activity often creates more impact than several cheaper options that underwhelm.

## **The Best Games for Team Building Events**

![](/images/fun-pro-uk-interactive-games.jpg)

[Interactive games](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/category/interactive-game-hire) consistently outperform passive activities when it comes to genuine team engagement. The competitive element creates natural conversation starters, whilst the shared experience of trying to beat a colleague's score builds connections more effectively than sitting through presentations together.

### **Reaction and Challenge Games**

Reaction-based games like the [Batak Lite](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/category/exhibition-games/61/batak-lite) and [Batak Pro](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/category/exhibition-games/43/batak-pro) have become fixtures at [corporate events](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/pages/promotional-event-games) for good reason. These games test speed and coordination through light-up targets that players must hit as quickly as possible, with scores displayed for all to see. The format works brilliantly for team building because it's immediately accessible, anyone can have a go, and the competitive element emerges naturally without requiring teams to be formally organised.

What makes reaction games particularly effective is the equalising nature of the challenge. Job titles and seniority become irrelevant when everyone is simply trying to improve their score. The managing director might find themselves outperformed by the newest graduate, which tends to generate exactly the kind of levelling interaction that strengthens team dynamics.

### **Racing and Driving Simulators**

[Racing car simulators](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/category/arcade-games/5/racing-car-simulators) tap into a near-universal appeal. Most people have some experience with driving games, which means the learning curve is gentle whilst the competitive potential remains high. Head-to-head races create natural drama, and lap times provide clear metrics for tournament formats.

For [team building](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/pages/team-building-ideas) purposes, racing simulators work particularly well because they accommodate different skill levels within the same activity. Casual participants enjoy the experience without pressure, whilst competitive colleagues can battle for fastest lap times. The spectator element also adds value, as watching colleagues compete generates energy throughout the venue.

### **Classic Arcade and Retro Games**

Retro [arcade machines](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/category/arcade-games/4/retro-arcade-games) serve as natural conversation starters at team building events. There's something about classic games that prompts people to share memories and compare experiences in ways that modern entertainment often doesn't. [Dance machines](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/category/arcade-games/39/dance-machine) add high-energy entertainment that draws crowds, whilst games like [Whack A Mole](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/category/arcade-games/67/whack-a-mole-game-hire) provide accessible fun that appeals across age groups.

The nostalgia factor shouldn't be underestimated. When a senior colleague starts reminiscing about the arcade they visited as a teenager, it creates common ground with younger team members in a way that work conversations rarely achieve.

### **Giant Games and Physical Challenges**

Oversized versions of classic games like [Giant Jenga](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/category/corporate-entertainment/44/giant-jenga-hire) and [Giant Connect 4](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/category/corporate-entertainment/45/giant-connect-4-game-hire) create spectacle whilst remaining genuinely strategic. These games work particularly well for team building because they slow the pace compared to electronic games, allowing for conversation and tactical discussion whilst still maintaining competitive tension.

Giant Jenga, in particular, generates memorable moments. The collective intake of breath as someone attempts a risky move, followed by either relief or dramatic collapse, creates shared experiences that teams reference long after the event.

### **Prize and Incentive Games**

[Prize crane](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/category/arcade-games/47/prize-crane-arcade-grabber) arcade grabbers and similar games add an extra dimension to team building events by combining skill with tangible rewards. These work especially well as part of a broader activity mix, giving people something to try between main events. When stocked with branded merchandise or desirable prizes, they maintain engagement throughout the day.

## **Team Building Events for Different Occasions**

The same games can serve different purposes depending on the context. Understanding how activities fit various event types helps with planning.

### **Conference energisers**

These events require games that work in short bursts without complex setup. Reaction challenges and arcade games excel here, providing a mental break between sessions whilst getting people out of their seats. The key is choosing activities that deliver immediate engagement, as attendees won't have time to learn complicated rules.

### **Away days and dedicated team building days**

These benefit from variety and rotation. Setting up multiple activity stations allows teams to move between games, preventing queues and ensuring everyone stays engaged. A mix of competitive games, skill challenges, and social activities creates a balanced programme.

### **Evening entertainment and corporate parties**

These types of events call for games that complement food and drinks in a more formal setting rather than dominating the agenda. Arcade games, giant games, and prize machines work well here, creating activity zones that people can drift between whilst socialising.
