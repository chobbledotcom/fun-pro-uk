## How to Hire Games for Your College Or School Summer Party

Finding and securing the right entertainment requires advance planning:

1.  Start early: Book popular items at least 2-3 months in advance, especially for June/July events when demand peaks
    
2.  Consider your budget: Quality game hire comes at various price points—establish your spending limit before contacting suppliers
    
3.  Think about space: Measure your available indoor and outdoor areas to ensure games will fit comfortably
    
4.  Check logistics: Confirm access routes, power requirements, and whether staff supervision is included
    
5.  Verify safety credentials: Ensure suppliers have proper insurance and safety certifications for school events

### Maximising Value When You Hire Games

Make your budget stretch further with these practical tips:

*   Book multiple games from a single supplier for potential package discounts
    
*   Consider half-day hire for shorter events
    
*   Share costs with other local schools or parent committees for consecutive day bookings
    
*   Balance premium attractions with more affordable options
    
*   Ask about term-time weekday discounts (often available outside peak weekend demand)

## Creating Themed Zones for Different Interests

Rather than distributing games randomly, consider creating themed activity zones that cater to different interests:

### Active Zone:

*   Inflatables and physical challenges
    
*   Sports-based competitions
    
*   Dance and movement games

### Skill Zone:

*   Target games requiring precision
    
*   Problem-solving challenges
    
*   Reaction testing games

### Chill-Out Zone:

*   Less intensive activities
    
*   Games suitable for quiet participation
    
*   Options for those needing sensory breaks

This approach ensures pupils can find activities that match their energy levels and preferences throughout the event.

## College and School Summer Party Games: Inclusivity Considerations

Ensuring all pupils can participate regardless of ability is essential:

*   Select games that can be enjoyed by children with different physical capabilities
    
*   Include options that don't rely solely on speed or strength
    
*   Consider noise levels for pupils with sensory sensitivities
    
*   Ensure adequate shaded areas for outdoor events
    
*   Provide seating near activities for those who need rest periods
