      ## **Planning Game Hire for Your Leeds Event**

      A little planning goes a long way with interactive games, and there are a few things it's worth thinking about early. 

      The first is space and access: it's worth checking your floor space, the power available and how equipment will get into the room. A ground-floor space with a loading bay, like New Dock Hall, makes life simple, whereas a city centre venue may need a delivery window agreed in advance. We'll always go through this with you so nothing catches you out on the day.

      It also helps to think about the mix of people attending. A corporate audience usually spans a wide range of ages, abilities and personalities, so a blend of higher-energy reaction games and lower-intensity options means everyone can find something they're comfortable joining in with. 

      Timing matters too: games earn their place differently depending on the moment. On an exhibition stand they're there to draw footfall and create dwell time for conversations, at an evening reception they break the ice, and at an all-day fun day they work best as stations guests rotate through.

      Finally, it's worth being clear on what you want the day to achieve. If the goal is footfall and lead capture at a trade show, branded prize and reaction games tend to deliver. If it's ++[team building](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/events/team-building-entertainment/)++, shared, sociable games work better, and if you simply want a relaxed atmosphere at a staff celebration, arcade and pub games do the job nicely. Tell us your objective and we'll recommend a mix that fits it.
