      ## **Leicester Venues We Know Well**


      Knowing a venue before you arrive makes the day run more smoothly. Loading
      access, floor surfaces, where the power points are, and the timing window
      you've got for setup all shape what's practical, and they vary a lot from
      one Leicester venue to the next. Here's how we think about some of the
      city's better-known spaces.


      ### **King Power Stadium**


      Home of Leicester City FC and a little over a mile from the city centre,
      King Power Stadium runs a wide range of event spaces, from intimate
      hospitality boxes through to larger suites built for conferences,
      exhibitions and dinners. The variety is the thing to plan around: a
      compact, self-contained game suits a hospitality box or a breakout suite,
      while arcade machines and simulators have room to breathe in the bigger
      halls. On-site parking is generous, which helps with load-in, though
      access needs pre-planning around fixtures, as the stadium isn't available
      in the usual way on match days.


      ### **Mattioli Woods Welford Road**


      The home of Leicester Tigers is one of the largest event venues in the
      city, with a substantial spread of event suites across the ground and the
      voco Leicester hotel right next door. The scale makes it well suited to
      exhibitions, larger conferences and award dinners, and it's a natural fit
      for branded exhibition games and footfall-driving prize games on a show
      floor. As with any stadium, it's worth confirming dates early and
      factoring in match-day access.


      ### **The Athena**


      A striking art deco building in the Cultural Quarter, the Athena is a
      popular choice for large corporate Christmas parties and award ceremonies,
      with space for sizeable banquet-style events. Its reception areas and main
      auditorium lend themselves to photo booths, arcade machines and reaction
      games run as part of a drinks reception or as post-dinner entertainment.

      ## **Planning Game Hire for a Leicester Corporate Event**


      A bit of early thought makes the entertainment side far easier to manage.
      These are the things we'd suggest considering before you book your
      Leicester game hire.


      Start with guest numbers and flow. Games need clear operating space, plus
      a little room for people to queue without blocking a walkway or a fire
      exit. Sharing a rough floor plan early lets us suggest a layout that keeps
      people moving rather than bunching up in one corner.


      Think about who's actually attending. A room full of competitive
      twenty-somethings calls for something different to a mixed-age team with a
      range of mobility and confidence levels. We'd usually recommend a spread
      of games rather than one high-octane option, so everyone has something
      they're comfortable joining in with.


      Be clear on what you want the entertainment to do. An exhibition stand
      needs games that draw footfall and engage people in seconds, because
      you've only got a moment to catch a passer-by. A ++[team
      building](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/events/corporate-events/)++ day is
      the opposite; there you want activities that hold attention for longer and
      naturally mix people who don't usually work together.


      Don't overlook the practical side. Most of our games run from a standard
      mains socket and need a couple of metres of clear floor, but the venue can
      complicate things. Stadium venues have plenty of parking yet restrict
      access on match days, and city centre venues often rely on public car
      parks such as NCP St Nicholas Circle for the Holiday Inn and Cathedral
      Quarter area. Flagging any unusual access or power constraints early saves
      surprises on the day.


      Lastly, if you'd like games branded with your logo, raise it as soon as
      you can. Because we design and print in-house, we can be flexible on
      timings, but custom work always benefits from a bit of lead time.
