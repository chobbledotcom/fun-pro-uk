      ## **Planning Game Hire for a Liverpool Corporate Event**


      Liverpool's major venues are well set up for entertainment, with good
      delivery access and experienced event teams, so the logistics are usually
      straightforward. The bigger decisions are about matching the entertainment
      to your event, and a few things are worth thinking about early.


      Start with guest numbers and flow. A game needs clear space to run safely,
      plus room for a short queue that won't block walkways or fire exits. If
      you can share a floor plan when you enquire, we can suggest where things
      sit.


      Think about the mix of people attending. A room full of different ages,
      abilities and personalities is better served by a range of options than a
      single high-energy game, so we'll usually suggest a couple of contrasting
      choices to make sure everyone can join in and enjoy themselves.


      Be clear on what you want the entertainment to achieve. An exhibition
      stand needs fast games that drive footfall and start conversations,
      whereas a ++[team building
      day](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/events/team-building-entertainment/)++
      benefits from activities that hold attention over a few hours. They call
      for different approaches, and naming the goal helps us recommend the right
      thing.


      On logistics, most of our games run from a standard mains socket and need
      a couple of metres of clear floor, but it's worth flagging anything
      unusual about your venue early. And if you'd like games
      ++[branded](https://www.funprouk.co.uk/promotional-event-games/)++ with
      your own design, raise it as soon as you can, since the design and print
      are handled in-house and production takes a little lead time.
